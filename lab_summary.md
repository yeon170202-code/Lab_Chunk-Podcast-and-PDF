After testing different chunking methods on our Trustworthy AI materials, I found that there isn't a "one-size-fits-all" solution. For the PDF document, the Recursive Character Chunking worked best because it was smart enough to stop at paragraphs, keeping the technical sections and headers together. For the Podcast transcript, Token-Based Chunking was the winner; since people talk in long, messy sentences, counting tokens ensured the AI could "read" the conversation without getting overwhelmed. The main trade-off I noticed is that while simpler methods like Fixed-Size are fast, they often cut a sentence right in the middle, which loses the emotional meaning of the message. In the end, choosing the right strategy is about balancing how much structure you want to keep versus how much data the AI can handle at once.
